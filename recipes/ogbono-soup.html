Simple Ogbono soup

Ogbono soup is a go-to Nigerian gooey soup made from dried Bush mango seeds. A lot of people may love Ogbono soup, but one mistake in the preparation could turn it to a mess. Here is my simple preparation method

Ingredients

  • 2 cups blended dried Ogbono
  • 500 g beef
  • 500 g tripe
  • 1 medium-sized dried fish or stockfish
  • 1/2 cup palm oil
  • 2 tablespoons blended crayfish
  • 1 tablespoon blended pepper
  • 1 small red onion
  • 2 Knorr cubes
  • 1 handful uziza leaves (or Ugu leaves)

Cooking Instructions

  1. Wash meat and season with 1 Knorr cube, the onion(chopped) and some Salt. Cook till done and set aside.
  2. Wash and cut uziza leaves (or leaves of choice) and set aside
  3. Place a pot over medium heat and add in the palm oil to heat up (don't bleach)
  4. Gradually add the grinded OGBONO into the hot palm oil while stirring it to fry for about 1 minute or 2. Gradually add in a cup of water to it and allow simmer for about a minute.
  5. Add the boiled meat, stockfish/dried fish, kpomo, and the other remaining ingredients (in no particular order) and then gradually stir and allow to boil for about 2-3minutes.
  6. Taste for salt before adding salt, then add the vegetable lastly.
  7. Allow simmer for another minute and put off cooking heat
  8. Serve with any swallow of your choice
